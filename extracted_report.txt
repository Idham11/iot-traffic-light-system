 ABSTRACT  
Malaysia is one of the countries  that is struggling with traffic congestion. The 
cause  of the traditional traffic light system is that it only relies  on fixed timers  without 
adapting to current vehicle flow,  leading to delays  in traffic. The overcome idea for 
these problems, this project proposes the development of an Internet of Things Traffic 
Light System that can optimise  the system to leverage current vehicle detection and 
remote monitoring capabilities. This system integrates a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W as  the 
main controller , an HLK -LD1125H radar sensor for detecting vehicles, and a 
Raspberry Pi Camera module for live video monitoring. Plus, a SIM7600E -H GSM 
module is act for transferring the data to a HTTPS website dashboard, allowing 
authorised  persons  to monitor traffic light status and the situation at the intersection 
remotely.  The system is planned to use the Waterfall model methodology, allowing 
stages from planning to testing. Key technologies for the frontend are HTML, CSS, 
and JavaScript, not forg etting TCP/IP for the network communication. This proposed 
solution aims to reduce the problem that has been faced for a decade by reducing 
waiting tim e.

INTRODUCTION  
1.1 Introduction  
Malaysia encounters significant traffic congestion, especially in urban areas in 
Johor Bahru, only dependent on private vehicles, with more than 36. 3 million 
registered vehicles, and the state's road network is saturated (BERNAMA, 2024) . This 
action impacts daily traffic jams, and government programs like fuel subsidies and 
accessible car financing increase the existing congestion. Moreover, the insufficient 
road expansion and ineffective traffic management systems intensify the issue, l eading 
to longer commute durations, heightened fuel usage, and financial losses.  
The rise of private car ownership, dragged by the insufficiency of public transit 
alternatives, was made worse by the enlarged population of Johor. Compared to Kuala 
Lumpur, where there are a variety of options for public transportation, such as MRT, 
LRT, KTM, and Rapid Bus, Johor only relies on buses as the main form of public 
transportation (CNA, 2025) . Because of this, people choose to drive and contribute to 
the traffic congestion.  
1.2 Problem Background  
T-junctions are arguably one of the most problematic locations , which cause 
congestion, the traffic signals running inefficiently like poorly timed systems, and 
traffic management strategies delaying the traffic advancements for reasons that 
cannot be justified . Moreover, if this problem continues, Johor will fail to manage its 
road traffic congestion in the future. It will also impact daily traffic if no public

While this is a problem for most larger towns or cities, small towns or surrounding

Observing an interview involving Mr. Mohd Yuzairi bin Md Yasir, the

ease the flow  in the Skudai area with a reasonable budget and development timeframe

In the Skudai area, IoT traffic lights using inductive loop sensors operate by 
placing these sensors under the pavement at intersections. When there is a vehicle 
passing through the loop, a shift in inductance occurs, signaling the presence of a 
vehicle. These lo ops transfer real -time information to a linked microcontroller of the 
central traffic management system. The system analyzes the information to assess how 
many vehicles are queued in a lane. The traffic light controller adjusts the timing of 
the gre en, yellow, and red signals to ease the traffic flow. An advanced system may 
also link to a cloud -based dashboard where data is recorded, which only allows

2.3 Current System Analysis  
Inductive loop sensors are commonly used in traffic light systems but face 
several problems. One of the major problems is that they can only detect metallic 
objects, which means they are nonfunctional when it comes to detecting non -metallic 

 
25 
 road users such as bicycles, lightweight plastic vehicles, and pedestrians. This reduces 
the accuracy of the system, especially in areas with diverse traffic. Moreover, vehicles 
must pass by the loop for detection to occur, or the system may assume that th e lane is 
empty and keep the light red unnecessarily.  
 
In addition, the inductive loop system is inflexible due to its dependency on 
permanent installations, which makes it unsuitable for prototypes, educational 
purposes, or temporary arrangements. The implementation process as a whole is often 
slow and requir es coordination with other departments, such as traffic control and civil 
engineering. Therefore, it will eventually increase the cost of reopening the road for 
maintenance is necessary and adding to the delays and taxing the city’s finances. This 
challeng e shows that we should look for more affordable and adaptable solutions.  
 
2.4 Comparison between existing systems  
Studying the current system before developing an IoT project is essential as it 
allows us to understand the technologies in use, limitations and applicability of the 
devices. By comparing systems based on technologies, design complexity, and cost, 
this project can be created with the most suitable elements while avoiding high -cost 
implementation unsuitable for small -scale, education al, or budget -sensitive 
applications.  
 
2.4.1 (Surtrac – Pittsburgh, USA)  
Surtrac is an intelligent traffic signal system developed by Carnegie Mellon 
University and has been commercialized by Rapid Flow Technologies. Traffic flow 
has been optimized by using artificial intelligence and sensors such as radar and 
cameras at the in tersection. Every intersection functions independently, gathering all 
the traffic information and modifying timing according to the immediate environment. 
This system is implemented in Pittsburgh and various other cities across the United 
States, aiming to  reduce traffic jams, enhance journey durations, and facilitate 
connected vehicle systems. This decentralized method of decision -making enables the 
 
26 
 system to adapt to the changing traffic situation without needing to centralize 
oversight.  
The system also has certain drawbacks due to its advanced technologies. The 
high cost of the system makes it impractical for a rural or reasonable budget area due 
to the dependable network infrastructure, high -resolution sensors, and sophisticated 
AI softw are. It requires an internet connection and poses additional difficulties. Surtrac 
is unique, that it can grow without the need for a large central server and is prepared 
to work with upcoming smart city technology like vehicle -to-infrastructure (V2I) 
comm unications (Miovision, 2025) . 
2.4.2 (Traffic Light Control Using Ultrasonic and PIR Sensors – Palembang, 
Indonesia)  
Researchers at Universitas Bina Darma in Palembang, Indonesia, have 
developed a smart traffic light control using both PIR motion sensors and Ultrasonic 
sensors to detect the presence of vehicles. This project is using Arduino Mega2560, 
aiming to enhance t he movement of vehicles at junctions by modifying the duration of 
traffic signals in real time based on current traffic. Making it special, because of the 
combination of PIR technology for detecting motion and ultrasonic to measure 
distance, will eventuall y improve the detection precision across different traffic light 
situations. This project is cost -effective, making it a feasible solution for smaller 
towns.  
 
In terms of advantages, the system enhances the efficiency of intersections, 
shortens the time waiting for vehicles, and the cost -effective by utilizing standard 
hardware components. It establishes the framework for upcoming improvements like 
centralized m anagement. On the downside, there were some difficulties with this 
project, ultrasonic sensors have a limited detection range and can the effected by 
weather conditions like fog, and it has not yet been tested on a large scale. Furthermore, 
without integra ting with the latest technologies like radar or AI -driven video analysis, 
the system may find it difficult to handle more complicated or busy intersections 
(Gusairi Rahman, 2025) .
